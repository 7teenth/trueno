Why TRUENO?
The protagonist of the Initial D anime and manga, Takumi Fujiwara, uses his father's AE86 Trueno GT-Apex hatchback to deliver tofu and race. Takumi's friend Itsuki Takeuchi drives an AE85 Levin. Throughout the series, two of Takumi's opponents drive AE86s: Wataru Akiyama drives a turbocharged AE86 Corolla Levin and Shinji Inui drives a coupe version of the AE86 Trueno. The AE86 is also an available vehicle in the Initial D Arcade Stage game series. In the movie The Fast and the Furious, a red Corolla AE86 can be seen waiting in line to enter the Race Wars racing festival. The AE86 also makes brief appearances in The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift and Fast and Furious 4. The popularity of the Initial D manga and anime has been cited as the main reason for the car's high value in the secondary market, often referred to as the "Takumi tax" or "tofu tax" after the protagonist and his occupation of delivering tofu.
In 2012, the Toyota 86 sports car was released, developed jointly by Toyota and Subaru and inspired by the AE86.
